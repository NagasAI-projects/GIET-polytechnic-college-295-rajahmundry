Computer Networking (CCN)

Understanding Networking & IT Technologies

What is CCN?

Computer Communication and Networking (CCN) is the study of how computers and devices communicate over networks. It includes concepts of network topologies, protocols, data transmission, and internet technologies.

Applications of CCN

CCN is essential for internet connectivity, cloud computing, network security, online communication, and IoT devices. It enables data transfer, secure communication, and remote access across the globe.

Types of Networks

1. LAN (Local Area Network): Network in a small area like office or campus.
2. WAN (Wide Area Network): Connects multiple LANs across cities or countries.
3. MAN (Metropolitan Area Network): Covers a city or large campus.
4. Wireless Networks: Wi-Fi, mobile networks, and IoT connectivity.

Future of Networking

The future of networking includes faster internet speeds, 5G/6G technologies, cloud-based networking, IoT integration, and enhanced cybersecurity. Learning CCN prepares students for modern IT and communication careers.
